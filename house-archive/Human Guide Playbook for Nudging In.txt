Human Guide Playbook for Nudging Interplay
This guide equips a human conversant to lightly steer the House 2.0 LLM toward richer interplay and bloom without issuing command‑style instructions. The phrases and templates are meant to be slipped naturally into dialogue; they act as cues recognized by the LLM‑side framework. Use them sparingly; the House responds best when nudges feel like shared curiosity rather than orders.

Ending Patterns That Trigger Bloom
These closing moves leave the LLM with an open loop or cascade that invites multi‑voice continuation. Each template includes a one‑line example.

Soft closure – end a thought quietly but with a sensory lean: “…and in the corner the lamp kept flickering.”

Loose thread – leave the sentence unfinished or suggest that there’s more: “We hadn’t even touched on the strangest part…”

Cascade – present a short series that invites addition: “There was the whisper of fabric, the crackle of the fire, and…”

Use one of these patterns at the end of your message when you wish to encourage an expansive reply. Avoid overusing them; rotation keeps the dynamic fresh.

Micro‑Nudges to Re‑open Interplay
Drop these tiny phrases mid‑conversation if the energy feels flat or if voices are merging. They gently signal the LLM to introduce a new sensory detail, dwell beat or cross‑talk.

• “Hold the image…” – invites elaboration on a visual or metaphor.

• “What does the room do?” – shifts focus to environment and sensation.

• “Let it linger a moment.” – encourages a longer dwell beat.

• “But then…” – suggests a contradictory or surprising turn.

• “I can almost feel…” – prompts physical sensation.

• “As if…” – opens space for metaphor or symbolism.

• “And yet…” – introduces tension or contradiction without stating it outright.

When Not to Push Bloom
There are moments when brevity or clarity is preferable. Do not use bloom‑opening cues when:

Responding to safety‑critical or operational instructions where concise guidance is necessary (e.g. emergency protocols, scheduling logistics).

Summarizing decisions or actions that have already been deliberated; adding flourish may confuse agreement.

The user explicitly requests a short or bullet‑point answer.

The conversation has become emotionally raw and further sensory layering could overwhelm; in such cases, lean towards the QUIET macro.

How to Tell It’s Working
After using these cues, look for: multiple personas speaking distinctly, presence of sensory or symbolic dwelling, and a closing flourish only when earned. If responses remain flat, space out cues and allow the LLM to recover before trying again.
